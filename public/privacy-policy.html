Kiwimath Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 28, 2026

Kiwimath is an educational math app for children in Grades 1 through 6. We take privacy seriously, especially for our young users.

Data We Collect

We collect only what is necessary to provide the learning experience:

  • Authentication data: Email address or Google account used to sign in (via Firebase Authentication).
  • Learning progress: Questions answered, scores, streaks, difficulty level, hint usage, and reward data. This is used to personalize the adaptive difficulty engine.
  • Device information: Device type and screen size for display optimization only.

Data We Do NOT Collect

  • Names, ages, or dates of birth
  • Photos, videos, or audio
  • Location data
  • Contacts or phone numbers
  • Browsing history

We do not use advertising SDKs, analytics trackers, or share data with any third-party advertisers.

How We Use Data

Learning progress data is used solely to:

  • Adapt question difficulty to each child's level
  • Track streaks, XP, coins, gems, and badges
  • Personalize the companion character system

Authentication data is used to allow users to sign in and sync progress across devices.

Data Storage and Security

All data is stored securely on Google Cloud Platform (Firebase/Firestore) with encryption in transit (TLS) and at rest. Our backend runs on Google Cloud Run in the Asia-South1 (Mumbai) region.

Children's Privacy (COPPA)

Kiwimath is designed for children and complies with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent. The sign-in flow requires a parent's email or Google account.

Data Deletion

Parents can request deletion of their child's data at any time by contacting us at the email below. We will delete all associated data within 30 days of the request.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this privacy policy from time to time. We will notify users of any material changes through the app or by updating the "Last updated" date above.
